Limitations

CME initial speed is estimated from flare GOES class (CDAW-catalog statistics), not measured by coronagraph — treat transit times as order-of-magnitude, not precise forecasts. No model can predict the exact moment a region will flare. Storm severity depends heavily on IMF Bz, which is only reliably knowable once a CME sheath reaches L1, roughly 30–60 minutes before Earth impact — a physical limit of the observing geometry, not a modeling gap. Aditya-L1/SoLEXS cross-validation uses raw total count rate vs. the day's median as an activity proxy, not a calibrated flux-to-GOES-class conversion, and Level-1 data has a multi-day publication latency (not real-time).
